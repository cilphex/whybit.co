Why Bitcoin

The year is 1998. You want to buy the new Massive Attack album, so you go to the store to purchase it. You exchange $20 for a plastic disc that you play in your car and in your room. You can't afford more, and if you don't have the disc you can't listen, but it's your only option for now.

The year is 1999. You want to buy the new Flaming Lips album, but you realize that Napster exists now. Instead of purchasing the album from a store that you drive to, you use the internet to download a digital copy, for free. You can download more whenever, from wherever, and you are content.

What you have experienced in the span of 1 year is a drastic change in one aspect of your life, made possible by the digitization of information.

Music is just information, and anything that is just information can be made digital. Things that are digital behave like light: they move fast, use little or no energy, and travel infinite distances.

This means that anything that is just information can be made to be fast, cheap, and global (cross-border, without restriction).

What is another example of something that is just information?

Before the internet, exchanging written information meant needing paper and envelopes, placing something in a mailbox or post office, paying for a stamp, and being uncertain about whether or not the mail would actually reach its destination.

But letters are just information. Email has made letters fast, cheap, and global. Emails are sent instantly instead of taking days. They cost nothing, instead of a stamp price. And they can be sent and read anywhere. (Unlike postal mail, which is blocked or scanned depending on the source and destination.)

Most practical accessories that people carry on their person are examples of things that are just information.

Your bank account, your credit cards. They keys to your house and car. The deeds to your house and your car. Your social security number, health insurance card, driver's license, work access card and the watch on your wrist.

Over time, all of these have or will fold into your phone. If you lose your wallet, these things take money and time to replace. If you lose your phone, they can be pulled from the cloud and synced to your new one for nothing.

All of these things are capable of being stored and sent anywhere, instantly, at no cost, because they are just information.


The year is 2017. You want to send money to your family in the developing world. You select a payment processor and pay a $5 fee to send $50. It takes a week before the payment returns as rejected. You try again, same result. After much effort, you eventually find a way, at great cost to your time, your wallet, and your family's fortitude.

The year is 2018...

You want to send money to your family in the developing world, and you realize that digital currency exists now. Instead of choosing a payment processor, you send bitcoin directly from your phone to your cousin's. The transfer registers in seconds, for pennies, cross-border.

What you have experienced in the span of 1 year is a drastic change in one aspect of your life, made possible by the digitization of information.

Money is just information, like music, movies, and news. It is just an accounting system, tracking value ownership claims. I have these tokens acting as claim to the output of the effort of others. She has more, they have less. It is just numbers attached to names. It is just information. It can be digitized. And it can move with the speed and effortlessness of light.


Bitcoin is to money what napster was to music, what bittorrent was to movies, what email was to communication, and what websites were to news.

Bitcoin is the informationization of money. It is the technology which moves this one aspect of our lives into something distributed and digital.

Bitcoin is money for the information age.

Now that it is here, we will never go back. All money will flow into digital currency because it is dramatically more efficient than all other forms of money. It is the socioeconomic path of least resistance.
