Why Bitcoin

The year is 1998. You want to buy the new Massive Attack album, so you go to the store to purchase it. You exchange $20 for a plastic disc that you play in your car and in your room, and you are content.

The year is 1999. You want to buy the new Flaming Lips album, but you realize that Napster exists now. Instead of driving to the store, you use the internet to download a digital copy for free, and you are content.

What you have experienced in the timespan of 1 year is the impact of digital networking on information. Music is just information, and anything that is just information can be made digital. Things that are digital behave like light: they move fast, use little or no energy, and travel infinite distances.

In practical terms, this means that anything that is just information can be made to be fast, cheap, and global (cross-border, without restriction).

What is another example of something that is just information?

Before the internet, exchanging written information meant needing paper and envelopes, placing something in a mailbox or post office, paying for a stamp, and being uncertain about delivery.

But letters are just information. After the dawn of the internet, email has made letters fast, cheap, and global. They are sent instantly instead of taking days. They cost nothing, instead of a stamp price. And they can be sent and read anywhere. (Unlike postal mail, which is prevented depending on the source and destination jurisdictions.)

Most practical accessories that people carry on their person are examples of things that are just information. Your bank account, your credit cards. They keys to your house and car. The deeds to your house and your car. Your social security number, health insurance card, driver's license, work access card and the watch on your wrist.

Over time, all of these have or will fold into your phone. If you lose your wallet, these things are gone. If you lose your phone, they can be synced to your new one. All of these things are capable of being sent anywhere, instantly, at no cost, because they are just information.


The year is 2008. You want to send money to your family in Cuba. You select a payment processor and pay a $5 fee to send $50. It takes a week before the payment returns as rejected. You try again, same result. After much effort, you eventually find a way, at a great cost to your time and your wallet.

But money is just information. Money is just an accounting system tracking value ownership claims. I have these tokens acting as claim to the output of the effort of others. She has more, they have less. Money is just numbers attached to names. It is just information.

The year is 2009. You want to send money to your family in Cuba, but you realize that Bitcoin exists now. Instead of choosing a payment processor, you send bitcoin directly from your home laptop to your cousin's. The transfer takes 10 minutes to confirm, and you are content.
